Electric Vehicle Summary Report Washington State

This report analyzes electric vehicle (EV) registration data from Washington State.

It includes Battery Electric Vehicles (BEVs) and Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicles (PHEVs).
The report shows top brands, price trends, electric range, and adoption across cities.

Dataset Description:-

  • It includes detailed information about each vehicles:

    • Brand (Make) and Model

    • Manufacturing year

    • Electric range and base price

    • CAFV (Clean Alternative Fuel Vehicle) eligibility

    • Geographic registration details like city, county, zip code, and GPS coordinates

    • Utility provider

    • Legislative district and census tract for policy-level insights

Datasource: https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/electric-vehicle-population-data

Column Name & Description

  • VIN (1-10): Vehicle Identification Number (partial, first 10 chars). Unique ID for each vehicle. Use for identifying duplicates or tracking individual entries.

  • County: The county where the vehicle is registered. Useful for regional analysis.

  • City: The city where the vehicle is registered. Useful to find top EV cities.

  • State: State abbreviation (always "WA" = Washington).

  • Postal Code: Area zip code. Can be used for location-level mapping or grouping.

  • Model Year: The year the EV was manufactured. Helpful in finding trends over years.

  • Make: The brand/company name (e.g., TESLA, FORD, NISSAN).

  • Model: Specific model name (e.g., Model 3, LEAF, Mustang Mach-E).

  • Electric Vehicle Type: EV type:

    • Battery Electric Vehicle (BEV) = fully electric
    • Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicle (PHEV) = electric + fuel

  • Clean Alternative Fuel Vehicle (CAFV) Eligibility: Whether the vehicle is eligible for the clean fuel program. Useful for policy eligibility analysis.

  • Electric Range: Maximum distance (in miles/km) the vehicle can travel on a full electric charge. Used to compare efficiency.

  • Base MSRP: Manufacturer Suggested Retail Price (initial cost of vehicle). Great for price comparison and budgeting.

  • Legislative District: WA state legislative district code. Useful for political/geographical mapping.

  • DOL Vehicle ID: Department of Licensing internal vehicle ID. Just a unique identifier (not useful for EDA).

  • Vehicle Location: Coordinates (longitude, latitude) where vehicle is registered. Useful for mapping on map/chart.

  • Electric Utility: The company supplying electricity to that region (e.g., PACIFICORP, PUGET SOUND ENERGY).

  • 2020 Census Tract: Geographical block code from 2020 US Census. Used in mapping/demographics.

Import library and Dataset

import pandas as pd
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt
import seaborn as sns
import warnings
%matplotlib inline
warnings.filterwarnings('ignore')

df = pd.read_csv("electric_data.csv")

This horizontal bar chart visualizes the top 10 electric vehicle (EV) brands registered in the state of Washington.

Each bar represents the number of vehicles registered under a specific brand.

  • TESLA leads by a significant margin, reflecting its strong presence and popularity in the EV market.

  • Chevrolet and Nissan follow as the next most popular EV brands, showing consistent adoption.

  • Other notable brands include Ford, BMW, and Toyota, indicating a diverse brand mix in the EV ecosystem.

The chart highlights the dominance of certain manufacturers and provides insight into consumer preferences in Washington's growing electric vehicle market.
top_makes = df["Make"].value_counts().head(10)

plt.figure(figsize=(10, 6))
sns.barplot(x=top_makes.values, y=top_makes.index, palette="viridis")
plt.title("Top 10 EV Brands in Washington")
plt.xlabel("Number of Vehicles")
plt.ylabel("Brand")
plt.show()

This line chart illustrates the year-wise trend of electric vehicle (EV) registrations in Washington state.

The X-axis represents the model year of the EVs, while the Y-axis shows the number of vehicles registered.

  • Key Observations:

Final Conclusion:

The electric vehicle landscape in Washington is rapidly evolving, with Tesla dominating in both registrations and range performance. EV adoption has grown significantly over recent years, particularly in urban regions like King County. However, a large portion of EVs are still not eligible for the CAFV program, highlighting a gap between environmental policy and vehicle capability. Overall, the data reveals increasing consumer interest, improved technology, and clear opportunities for infrastructure and policy improvements to support future EV growth.
